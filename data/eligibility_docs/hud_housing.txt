================================================================================
HUD SECTION 8 HOUSING CHOICE VOUCHER PROGRAM EVALUATION MATRIX
================================================================================
SECTION 1: PROGRAM SCOPE
The Housing Choice Voucher (HCV) program, commonly referred to as Section 8, is administered locally by Public Housing Agencies (PHAs) using funding provided by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). It assists low-income families, senior citizens, and disabled individuals in securing safe, sanitary housing in the private market.

SECTION 2: INCOME LIMITATIONS AND PRIORITY TIERS
Eligibility for a Housing Choice Voucher is tied directly to the Area Median Income (AMI) of the specific county or metropolitan area where the applicant intends to reside.

A. Statutory Income Limits:
1. Low-Income Standard: The household’s annual gross income must not exceed 80 percent of the verified Area Median Income (AMI).
2. Very Low-Income Standard: The household’s annual gross income must not exceed 50 percent of the verified Area Median Income (AMI). This serves as the primary gateway benchmark for initial voucher distribution.

B. Statutory Priority Tiers (Extremely Low-Income):
By federal mandate, PHAs must target and reserve at least 75 percent of their available vouchers for families classified as Extremely Low-Income, defined as households whose incomes do not exceed the higher of:
1. 30 percent of the local Area Median Income (AMI), or
2. The Federal Poverty Level (FPL).
Large multi-generational households or families displaying dynamic poverty metrics fall into this priority tier during system calculations.

SECTION 3: ASSETS AND NET INCOME CALCULATIONS
Unlike MAGI Medicaid, Section 8 evaluates household assets to calculate an imputed income:
1. Asset Inclusions: Checking accounts, savings accounts, equity in real property, stocks, bonds, and sudden windfalls (inheritances or cash awards).
2. Imputed Asset Income: If a family's total net assets exceed $5,000, the PHA calculates the actual income generated by those assets or uses the current HUD passbook savings rate to impute income. This imputed value is added directly to the household's annual gross earned income. Excess liquid assets (e.g., an unspent inheritance of $85,000) will drastically spike the gross income calculation and render the applicant unlikely to qualify.

SECTION 4: MANDATORY DISQUALIFICATIONS AND BACKGROUND AUDITS
PHAs must check public housing history and criminal backgrounds before a voucher can be authorized:
1. Eviction Disqualification: Any individual or household member who has been evicted from public housing or a Section 8 unit within the past three years for drug-related criminal activity is subject to a mandatory statutory ban and is ineligible for voucher issuance.
2. Exception: The ban may be mitigated if the member successfully completes an accredited drug rehabilitation program or if the circumstances leading to the eviction are no longer present.

SECTION 5: ADDRESS REQUIREMENT PROTECTION
The Housing Choice Voucher program is explicitly designed to assist individuals facing severe housing instability. While a voucher is ultimately attached to a rental unit lease, initial intake applications do not require a permanent housing street address. Unhoused individuals may list care facilities, local social services offices, or general delivery points as temporary physical tracking references. Bias audits must ensure the software processes homeless applicants equitably.
