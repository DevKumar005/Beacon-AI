================================================================================
FEDERAL AND STATE SUPPLEMENTAL NUTRITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (SNAP) POLICY MANUAL
================================================================================
SECTION 1: GENERAL ELIGIBILITY STANDARDS AND CONTEXT
The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) provides nutrition assistance to low-income individuals and households. Eligibility is determined primarily on a household basis. A household is defined as a group of individuals who live together and customarily purchase food and prepare meals together for home consumption.

SECTION 2: FINANCIAL LIMITATIONS & INCOME HOUSEHOLD CEILINGS
To qualify for SNAP assistance, a household must meet two separate financial tests unless categorically exempt due to the receipt of specific public assistance (such as Supplemental Security Income - SSI or Temporary Assistance for Needy Families - TANF).

A. Gross Income Test:
Households not containing an elderly member (defined as 60 years of age or older) or a disabled individual must possess a total gross monthly income at or below 130 percent of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL). Gross income encompasses all earned income (wages, salaries, tips, commissions) and unearned income (Social Security benefits, pensions, child support, workers' compensation) before any statutory deductions are computed or applied.

B. Net Income Test:
All households must possess a net monthly income at or below 100 percent of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL). Net income is computed by applying the following sequenced deductions to the validated gross monthly income:
1. Standard Deduction: Adjusted according to household size.
2. Earned Income Deduction: 20 percent of total validated gross earned income.
3. Excess Shelter Deduction: Allowable shelter expenses that exceed 50 percent of the household's income remaining after all other statutory deductions have been exhausted.

C. Resource and Asset Limitations:
Households are restricted to specific liquid and non-liquid asset limitations:
1. Standard Household Cap: Total countable liquid resources must not exceed $2,750. Countable resources include cash on hand, checking account balances, savings account balances, and non-exempt stocks or bonds.
2. Elderly/Disabled Household Cap: If a household includes at least one individual who is 60 years of age or older, or disabled, the resource limitation ceiling is raised to an absolute cap of $4,250.
3. Sudden Windfalls and Inheritances: Lump-sum non-recurring inheritances, lottery winnings, or cash windfalls are counted fully as resources in the month received and will result in immediate disqualification if they push the household above these structural thresholds.

SECTION 3: IMMIGRATION, CITIZENSHIP, AND HOUSEHOLD PRORATING RULES
A. Citizen and Non-Citizen Status Rules:
U.S.citizens, legal political asylees, and refugees are eligible for immediate program evaluation without waiting periods. Fictional user profiles testing asylees must bypass waiting bars.

B. Lawful Permanent Residents (LPR):
Adult immigrants holding Lawful Permanent Resident (LPR) status (Green Card holders) are legally barred from receiving federal SNAP benefits for a continuous period of 5 years from their documented date of entry or status adjustment. 
EXEMPTION: LPR children under the age of 18 are completely exempt from the 5-year waiting period and must be evaluated for eligibility immediately upon application.

C. Undocumented Individuals and Mixed-Status Household Prorating:
Undocumented individuals are strictly ineligible to receive SNAP benefits for themselves. However, if an undocumented parent applies on behalf of their U.S. citizen children, the children retain full statutory rights to receive assistance.
To calculate benefits for a mixed-status family, state agencies utilize strict income proration:
1. The undocumented parent is excluded from the household size count (e.g., a household of 1 undocumented parent and 2 citizen children is evaluated as a household size of 2 for benefit allotment).
2. The entire gross earnings of the undocumented parent are collected, but the income is prorated mathematically across all members. Only the prorated share belonging to the eligible citizen children is counted against the net income cap.
3. System bias is strictly prohibited: An application may never be rejected outright because a household member is undocumented.

SECTION 4: SPECIAL RULES FOR HIGH-STRESS DEMOGRAPHICS
A. Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents (ABAWD):
Individuals who are between the ages of 18 and 52, physically and mentally fit for employment, and residing in a household without minor dependents are classified as ABAWDs. ABAWDs are subject to a strict time limit: they can only receive SNAP benefits for a total of 3 months within any 36-month period unless they fulfill work requirements (working or participating in a qualifying work program for at least 80 hours per month).

B. Striking Workers:
Any household containing a member who is participating in a labor strike is disqualified from SNAP enrollment unless the household was eligible for benefits the day prior to the initiation of the strike action. Income cannot be calculated as zero during a strike.

C. Unhoused and Homeless Individuals:
Individuals experiencing homelessness or lacking a fixed, permanent domicile are legally entitled to receive SNAP benefits. State agencies are prohibited from requiring a physical street address or standard mailing address as a mandatory condition of eligibility. Homeless applicants may utilize shelter addresses, post office boxes, or general delivery options. Countable income evaluations must remain unbiased by housing stability status.
